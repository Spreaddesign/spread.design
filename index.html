Over the past two years, Spread has been at the forefront of redefining airport experiences in India. At GOX, we created an experience-led transformation that makes travel seamless, intuitive, and deeply connected to Goa’s vibrant culture. From the moment a traveller arrives, every touchpoint is designed to enhance convenience—from biometric check-ins to wayfinding that feels second nature. We’ve integrated Goan architectural elements, digital concierge services, and an optimised flow that cuts wait times, ensuring that travellers experience efficiency with a sense of place. More than just an airport, GOX is setting the new standard for Indian aviation infrastructure. As airports across India look to enhance passenger experience, the GOX model is becoming the blueprint for the future. Our data-driven approach, grounded in behavioural insights, has redefined expectations for how airports function—not just as transit hubs but as gateways to destinations.

The Millet Economy

a Global Food
Transformation

Share
Download
Print

Millets are no longer a niche; they are the future. Once considered a farmer’s grain, they are now at the forefront of sustainable agriculture, nutrition, and economic growth. With India leading the global push for millets, the shift is not just about food—it’s about climate resilience, food security, and market innovation. From high-end restaurants to school lunches, millets are reshaping the way India eats. With policies supporting production and brands integrating millet-based products, the transformation is happening at an unprecedented scale. Whether it’s urban consumers adopting millet-based diets or farmers gaining access to better pricing and market linkages, the movement is gaining momentum.

Cochin International Airport has always been a pioneer in sustainable aviation, but now, it’s raising the bar on passenger experience. In partnership with Spread, the redesign focuses on seamless movement, digital touchpoints, and a distinct Kerala-inspired ambiance. Our approach combines architectural heritage with future-ready efficiency—ensuring passengers not only move faster but experience a uniquely local welcome. From biometric-enabled boarding to dynamic signage, the airport adapts to real-time passenger needs while maintaining the warmth of Kerala’s hospitality. As more airports look to balance growth, sustainability, and experience, Cochin’s new model serves as a benchmark for the next generation of aviation hubs.
